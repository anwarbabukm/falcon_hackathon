### The French Revolution: Causes, Major Events, and Outcomes

#### Causes of the French Revolution

1. **Social Inequality**
   - Division of society into three estates: 
     - **First Estate**: Clergy
     - **Second Estate**: Nobility
     - **Third Estate**: Commoners (bourgeoisie, workers, peasants)
   - Disparities in privileges, taxes, and rights.

2. **Economic Troubles**
   - Financial crisis due to debt from wars (e.g., American Revolution) and royal extravagance.
   - Heavy taxation on the Third Estate.
   - Poor harvests leading to food shortages and high bread prices.

3. **Political Factors**
   - Weak leadership of King Louis XVI.
   - Inefficiency and corruption within the government.
   - Enlightenment ideas promoting liberty, equality, and fraternity.

4. **Intellectual Influences**
   - Enlightenment philosophers (e.g., Voltaire, Rousseau, Montesquieu) questioning traditional authority and advocating for democratic principles.
   - Spread of revolutionary ideas through salons and pamphlets.

5. **Immediate Triggers**
   - Estates-General convened in May 1789 to address the financial crisis, leading to the formation of the National Assembly by the Third Estate.
   - Storming of the Bastille on July 14, 1789, symbolizing the uprising against tyranny.

#### Major Events of the French Revolution

1. **Formation of the National Assembly (June 1789)**
   - Third Estate declares itself the National Assembly, representing the people of France.
   - Tennis Court Oath: Vow to draft a new constitution.

2. **The Fall of the Bastille (July 14, 1789)**
   - Parisians storm the Bastille prison, marking the beginning of the revolution.

3. **The Great Fear (Summer 1789)**
   - Peasant revolts across the countryside due to rumors of aristocratic conspiracies.

4. **Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen (August 1789)**
   - National Assembly issues a document outlining individual and collective rights.

5. **Women's March on Versailles (October 1789)**
   - Women march to Versailles demanding bread and force the royal family to move to Paris.

6. **Reign of Terror (1793-1794)**
   - Period of radicalization led by Robespierre and the Committee of Public Safety.
   - Mass executions by guillotine, including King Louis XVI and Queen Marie Antoinette.

7. **Rise of Napoleon Bonaparte (1799)**
   - Coup d'état of 18 Brumaire, leading to Napoleon's rise to power as First Consul.

#### Outcomes of the French Revolution

1. **End of the Monarchy**
   - Abolition of the absolute monarchy and execution of King Louis XVI.

2. **Rise of the Republic**
   - Establishment of the First French Republic in 1792.

3. **Social Reforms**
   - Abolition of feudal privileges.
   - Reforms in laws, education, and administration.

4. **Spread of Revolutionary Ideas**
   - Influence on subsequent revolutions and democratic movements worldwide.
   - Promotion of nationalism and civic rights.

5. **Economic Changes**
   - Redistribution of land from the Church and nobility to peasants.
   - Rise of capitalism and decline of feudal economic structures.

6. **Impact on the Church**
   - Secularization of the state and reduction of the Church’s power.
   - Confiscation of Church lands and civil constitution of the clergy.

7. **Napoleonic Era**
   - Consolidation of revolutionary gains under Napoleon’s rule.
   - Spread of Napoleonic Code and revolutionary principles across Europe.

These notes provide a concise overview of the causes, major events, and outcomes of the French Revolution, tailored for a 10th grade student preparing for exams.
