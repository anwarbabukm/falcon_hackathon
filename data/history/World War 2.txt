### World War II: Events, Key Figures, and Aftermath

#### Events of World War II

1. **Invasion of Poland (1939)**
   - Germany invades Poland on September 1, 1939.
   - Britain and France declare war on Germany, marking the start of World War II.

2. **Fall of France (1940)**
   - Germany's Blitzkrieg tactics quickly overwhelm France.
   - France surrenders in June 1940; Germany occupies northern France.

3. **Battle of Britain (1940)**
   - Germany's Luftwaffe attempts to gain air superiority over Britain.
   - British RAF defends successfully, preventing German invasion.

4. **Operation Barbarossa (1941)**
   - Germany invades the Soviet Union in June 1941.
   - Initial successes, but ultimately fails due to harsh winter and Soviet resilience.

5. **Pearl Harbor (1941)**
   - Japan attacks US naval base at Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941.
   - US enters the war, joining the Allies.

6. **Battle of Stalingrad (1942-1943)**
   - Turning point on the Eastern Front.
   - Soviet Union defeats Germany after brutal urban warfare.

7. **D-Day (1944)**
   - Allied invasion of Normandy, France on June 6, 1944.
   - Opens a Western Front, leading to the liberation of Western Europe.

8. **Atomic Bombs (1945)**
   - US drops atomic bombs on Hiroshima (August 6) and Nagasaki (August 9).
   - Japan surrenders on August 15, 1945, ending the war.

#### Key Figures of World War II

1. **Adolf Hitler**
   - Dictator of Nazi Germany.
   - Initiated the war with aggressive expansion and the Holocaust.

2. **Winston Churchill**
   - Prime Minister of the United Kingdom.
   - Known for his leadership and stirring speeches.

3. **Joseph Stalin**
   - Leader of the Soviet Union.
   - Oversaw the defense of the Soviet Union and eventual counter-offensives.

4. **Franklin D. Roosevelt**
   - President of the United States.
   - Led the US through the Great Depression and most of World War II.

5. **Benito Mussolini**
   - Fascist leader of Italy.
   - Allied with Nazi Germany, later overthrown and executed.

7. **Dwight D. Eisenhower**
   - Supreme Allied Commander in Europe.
   - Planned and executed D-Day.

8. **Douglas MacArthur**
   - US General in the Pacific Theater.
   - Key figure in the liberation of the Philippines and Japan's surrender.

#### Aftermath of World War II

1. **Human Cost**
   - Estimated 70-85 million fatalities, including civilians and military personnel.
   - Holocaust: Six million Jews killed, along with other targeted groups.

2. **Political Changes**
   - Fall of Nazi Germany, Fascist Italy, and Imperial Japan.
   - Nuremberg Trials: Prosecution of Nazi war criminals.

3. **Economic Impact**
   - Devastation in Europe and Asia; extensive reconstruction needed.
   - Marshall Plan: US aid to rebuild Western Europe.

4. **Formation of the United Nations**
   - Established in 1945 to promote international cooperation and prevent future conflicts.

5. **Cold War**
   - Emergence of the US and the Soviet Union as superpowers.
   - Ideological, political, and military tension between the capitalist West and communist East.

6. **Decolonization**
   - Collapse of European colonial empires.
   - Independence movements in Asia, Africa, and the Middle East.

7. **Technological and Military Advances**
   - Development and use of atomic bombs.
   - Advancements in radar, jet engines, and medical technologies.

8. **Geopolitical Changes**
   - Division of Germany and Berlin into occupied zones.
   - Creation of Israel in 1948.
